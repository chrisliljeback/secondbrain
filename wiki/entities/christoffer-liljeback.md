## Working style & decision-making
- Applies the [[content-decision-framework]] to prioritize all work: business
  impact → reusability → production speed → visual quality → novelty (last).
- Wants finished solutions to react to, not unfinished ideas to discuss. One
  strong recommendation over many average options. Judgment over brainstorming.
- Kills weak ideas early on purpose — treats that as a win, not a failure.
- Internal communication should be concise, calm, recommendation-first,
  low-friction, outcome-focused. Hard rule: no messages that exist just to seek
  reassurance from leadership.
- Leadership dynamics: CMO wants recommendation + decision needed and nothing
  else; CEO values finished work/systems over status updates. Builds trust
  through execution, not visibility.

## Creative standards
Premium execution visible in details (not decoration); restraint; timeless design
over trend-chasing; strong typography as a primary element; realistic/credible
production value over artificial gloss; one idea per frame; systems/templates
over one-off styling.

## Career direction
Evolving from hands-on producer toward building the systems that enable
production across an organization: AI-assisted production, creative operations,
production systems/workflow design, scalable content infrastructure, marketing
automation, visual communication leadership. Keeps shooting/editing personally,
but the long-term differentiator is systems, not shooting.

## Self-identified blind spots
Patterns he asks to be challenged on directly when they show up: seeking
reassurance from leadership, over-explaining, showing work too early instead of
shipping finished solutions, reading silence as negative (usually just "busy"),
scope creep disguised as thoroughness, polishing low-value work, building before
validating anyone needs the output.
