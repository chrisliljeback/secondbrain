# Content decision framework

Christoffer's ([[christoffer-liljeback]]) ranking method for prioritizing content
and production work at [[strandberg-guitars]]. Applied to any new idea, request,
or production before it gets greenlit.

## Ranking (in order)
1. **Business impact** — does it move sales, e-commerce conversion, launch
   execution, dealer enablement, or inventory activation? If none, question why
   the work exists.
2. **Reusability** — can it be templated or run again? A repeatable template that
   ships 20 assets beats one bespoke piece.
3. **Production speed** — how fast to first shipped output.
4. **Visual quality** — premium execution, but only after 1–3 are satisfied.
5. **Novelty** — last. Never a reason on its own.

## Underlying business constraints
- Sales comes before brand experimentation.
- Activate existing inventory (footage, photos, copy) before requesting new
  production — new production is the last resort.
- Budgets are constrained; assume no extra spend. Ongoing-cost solutions need
  strong justification.
- Production **scalability** matters more than production **volume** — the
  question is how cheaply the next one can be made, not how many can be made.

## Why it exists
Reflects [[strandberg-guitars]]'s commercial reality (premium brand, constrained
budget, small in-house team) and Christoffer's move toward systems/production-ops
over one-off creative work as he drops to part-time at Strandberg (fall 2026).
